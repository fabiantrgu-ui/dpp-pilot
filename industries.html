Lumen Apparel · Our industry

Clothing has a transparency problem. We chose to be part of the fix.

Textiles are among the most resource-intensive product groups in Europe — which is why the EU named them a priority in its first Ecodesign Working Plan. We think the industry's biggest gap isn't ambition. It's information.

Why the industry struggles

Supply chains lose the story

A single tee passes through many hands across several countries — from fibre to spinning to dyeing to sewing. Each actor typically knows only its direct suppliers, so by the time a garment reaches you, most of its history is gone.

Sorters can't see inside fabric

At end of life, recyclers have to guess what a garment is made of — care labels wear off, and scanners struggle with blends and dark colours. Garments that could become new fabric end up burned or buried instead.

Claims outrun proof

"Green", "conscious", "responsible" — words are cheap when nobody can check them. Without verifiable data, honest brands and greenwashing look identical on a shop floor.

The EU's answer is the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR): from around 2027, every textile garment sold in the EU will need a Digital Product Passport. We're not waiting for the deadline.

Our commitments

Six things we hold ourselves to — all verifiable in the passport.

01

A passport on every garment

The Organic Tee carries one today as our pilot; the full range follows before the 2027 mandate. Not because we have to yet — because you should be able to check us now. See the live passport →

02

Certified materials, not adjectives

Our organic cotton is GOTS-certified and our recycled polyester is GRS-certified. The certificates are filed in the passport where auditors — and you — can find them.

03

Durability we test, not promise

Every model is tested for shape, shrinkage and twist to ISO methods, and the results are published — including the batch-level numbers regulators can re-check.

04

Repair before replace

Free reweave service in the first year and a repair contact printed in every passport. A garment that lasts twice as long halves its footprint — no new technology required.

05

Designed to be recycled

The Organic Tee is built as a mono-material garment — Class A recyclability — with disassembly notes in the passport so sorters know exactly what to do with it.

06

Named factories

Our manufacturing facility in Porto is identified in the passport by its global location number — not hidden behind "made in EU".

Where we're not there yet

Our footprint is Class C. We publish it anyway.

The Organic Tee's carbon footprint currently sits mid-scale on the EU's PEFCR rating. We could stay quiet about that until it improves — plenty do. We'd rather show you the honest number, and let you watch it move as we shift more volume to sea freight, renewable-powered mills and higher recycled content.

Don't take our word for any of this.
