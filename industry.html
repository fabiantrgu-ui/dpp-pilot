Lumen Apparel · Our industry

Clothing's biggest challenges are structural. So Lumen changed the structure.

Fashion's footprint problem starts with how the industry is built: garments cross continents through many hands, information is lost at every step, and claims can't be checked. Lumen Apparel counters this where it matters most — the supply chain itself.

The measures
01

A radically shortened supply chain

Spinning, knitting, dyeing and sewing for the Organic Tee all happen within a 90-kilometre radius in northern Portugal. Fewer tiers means fewer unknowns, shorter transport legs, and a supply chain Lumen can actually vouch for — facility by facility, each identified in the passport.

02

Materials with certificates, not adjectives

GOTS-certified organic cotton and GRS-certified recycled polyester. The certificates are filed in the Digital Product Passport where auditors — and customers — can check them.

03

Built to stay in use

Durability tested to ISO methods, a 24-month warranty and a free repair service in the first year. The garment that lasts twice as long roughly halves its footprint — no new technology required.

04

Designed for the next life

Mono-material construction earns the tee a Class A recyclability rating, and disassembly notes in the passport tell sorters exactly how to process it.

05

Transparency before it's mandatory

Every data point behind these measures lives in the Digital Product Passport — launched as a pilot years ahead of the EU's 2027 requirement. See it live →
