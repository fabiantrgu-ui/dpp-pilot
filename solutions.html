Lumen Apparel · Transparency Portal

Your clothes have a story. Now you can read it.

Every Lumen garment carries a Digital Product Passport — a small tag with a big answer. Scan it and see what your tee is made of, who made it, how it was tested, and where it should go when you're done wearing it.

What you'll see when you scan

What it's really made of

The exact fibre mix — like 80% certified organic cotton and 20% recycled polyester — backed by certificates, not just a label.

Its honest footprint

The carbon and environmental footprint class of your garment, calculated by the EU's own method — so you can compare across brands.

How to make it last

Care instructions that actually protect the fabric, plus our free repair service — because the most sustainable tee is the one you keep.

Where it goes next

Recyclability rating and take-back guidance, so your tee becomes new fabric instead of landfill when its time comes.

Why trust it

Claims are easy. Proof is better.

"Sustainable" is printed on a lot of labels. A Digital Product Passport is different: it's a structured record required by EU law (the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation), and the figures you see are the same ones market surveillance authorities can audit. Our organic and recycled claims are third-party certified — GOTS and GRS — and the certificates sit in the same passport.

What you see is what the regulator sees, minus only commercially confidential detail. That's the point: transparency you don't have to take on faith.

How it works

Find the tag

Every Lumen garment has a QR code on the care label and swing tag — it stays with the garment for life.

Scan with your phone

Any camera app works. No app to install, no account to create, nothing to pay.

Read the passport

The full record opens in your browser, in plain language — from fibres to footprint to what to do at end of life.

Where we are today: the passport is live as a pilot on the Lumen Organic Tee. Every garment in our range will carry one before the EU mandate arrives in 2027.
