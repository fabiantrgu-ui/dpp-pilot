What is a Digital Product Passport?

A Digital Product Passport (DPP) is a digital record attached to a physical product — opened by scanning the tag. It tells you what the garment is made of, who made it, how it was tested and what should happen to it at end of life. From 2027, EU law will require one for every textile product sold in Europe.

More transparency

The passport shows far more than a sewn-in label ever could — fibres, factories, test results and footprint, all in one scan.

Know what you're buying

Real detail before you decide: where the cotton is certified organic, how much of the fabric is recycled, and how durable the garment tested.

Less greenwashing

Every claim in the passport is backed by a standard, a test method or a third-party certificate — and it's the same record regulators can audit.

Trust & reliability

Data comes from verified suppliers and accredited test labs, is kept current over the product's life, and stays accessible free of charge.

These are the values Lumen Apparel stands for. Transparency, honest detail and verifiable claims are how a linear industry becomes a circular one — and the passport is how we prove we mean it.
